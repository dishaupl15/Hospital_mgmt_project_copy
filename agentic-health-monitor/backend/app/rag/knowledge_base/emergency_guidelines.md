## URGENCY LEVEL: IMMEDIATE (Call Emergency Services — 911)

### Pattern: Stroke / TIA
- Emergency Trigger: sudden face drooping + arm weakness + speech difficulty (any one of FAST signs)
- Additional Signs: sudden severe headache, sudden vision loss in one eye, sudden loss of balance or coordination
- Time Sensitivity: treatment window is 4.5 hours for thrombolysis — every minute counts
- Action: call 911 immediately, note exact time symptoms started, do not give food or water
- Do Not: drive patient yourself, wait for symptoms to improve spontaneously

### Pattern: Heart Attack (MI)
- Emergency Trigger: crushing central chest pain lasting more than 5 minutes + shortness of breath + sweating + nausea
- Additional Signs: pain radiating to left arm, jaw, or neck; sense of impending doom; cold clammy skin
- Time Sensitivity: door-to-balloon time target is 90 minutes — immediate intervention saves heart muscle
- Action: call 911, chew 325mg aspirin if no allergy, rest completely, loosen tight clothing
- Do Not: drive to hospital, eat or drink anything, exert yourself

### Pattern: Anaphylaxis
- Emergency Trigger: sudden allergic reaction with throat swelling + breathing difficulty + hives + drop in blood pressure
- Additional Signs: exposure to known allergen (bee sting, food, medication), rapid pulse, vomiting
- Time Sensitivity: epinephrine within minutes prevents death
- Action: use epinephrine auto-injector (EpiPen) if available, call 911, lie flat with legs elevated
- Do Not: give antihistamine as sole treatment, allow patient to stand if feeling faint

### Pattern: Sepsis
- Emergency Trigger: infection source + altered mental status + rapid breathing (over 22/min) + low blood pressure (under 90 systolic)
- Additional Signs: fever above 38.5°C or hypothermia below 36°C, rapid heart rate over 90, decreased urine output
- Time Sensitivity: every hour of delay in antibiotics increases mortality by 7%
- Action: call 911, note all symptoms and time of onset, do not delay for oral treatment
- Do Not: wait to see if patient improves with home remedies

### Pattern: Pulmonary Embolism
- Emergency Trigger: sudden severe shortness of breath + sharp chest pain worsening with breathing + rapid heart rate + coughing blood
- Additional Signs: recent prolonged immobility or surgery, one leg swollen and painful (DVT source), low oxygen saturation
- Time Sensitivity: massive PE can cause sudden death within minutes
- Action: call 911, keep patient calm and still, do not allow exertion
- Do Not: give anticoagulants without medical supervision

### Pattern: Meningococcal Septicemia
- Emergency Trigger: fever + non-blanching petechial or purpuric rash + altered consciousness
- Glass Test: press glass against rash — if rash does not fade, it is non-blanching — medical emergency
- Time Sensitivity: can progress from rash to death in hours
- Action: call 911 immediately, do not wait for rash to spread

### Pattern: Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA)
- Emergency Trigger: known or suspected diabetes + vomiting + fruity breath + rapid deep breathing (Kussmaul) + confusion
- Additional Signs: blood sugar over 250 mg/dL with ketones in urine, severe dehydration
- Action: call 911, do not give insulin without medical supervision for severe cases

## URGENCY LEVEL: HIGH (Emergency Department — Within 2 Hours)

### Pattern: Unstable Angina
- Trigger: chest pain at rest or with minimal exertion, not relieved by nitroglycerin, new onset severe chest pain
- Action: go to emergency department immediately, do not wait for scheduled appointment

### Pattern: Severe Asthma Attack
- Trigger: severe breathing difficulty not relieved after 2 doses of rescue inhaler, unable to speak in full sentences, silent chest on examination (no wheeze — severe)
- Action: emergency department immediately, continue rescue inhaler on the way

### Pattern: Appendicitis
- Trigger: progressive right lower quadrant pain with fever, nausea, and rebound tenderness
- Action: emergency department — surgical evaluation required, do not eat or drink

### Pattern: Hypertensive Crisis
- Trigger: blood pressure above 180/120 with symptoms (headache, visual changes, chest pain, confusion, nausea)
- Action: emergency department for symptomatic hypertensive emergency

### Pattern: Severe Hypoglycemia
- Trigger: blood sugar below 50 mg/dL OR confusion/loss of consciousness in diabetic patient
- Action: if conscious — 15g fast-acting carbohydrate; if unconscious — call 911, do not give anything by mouth

## URGENCY LEVEL: WITHIN 24 HOURS (Urgent Care or Same-Day Appointment)

### Conditions Requiring Prompt Evaluation
- New onset jaundice in adults
- Fever above 39°C lasting more than 48 hours
- Single FAST symptom that resolved (possible TIA)
- New onset severe headache without red flags
- Significant leg swelling with calf pain
- Blood in urine or stools
- Acute joint swelling with fever (possible septic arthritis)
- Chest pain atypical for cardiac with ongoing concern

---

## URGENCY LEVEL: WITHIN 3 DAYS (Scheduled Appointment)

### Conditions Requiring Timely Evaluation
- Persistent fever below 39°C without improvement
- Recurrent headaches affecting daily activity
- Moderate abdominal pain without red flags
- New skin rash without systemic symptoms
- Mild swelling or joint pain after minor injury
- Fatigue and weakness lasting more than 2 weeks
- Persistent nausea or decreased appetite

---

## URGENCY LEVEL: ROUTINE (Self-Care + Monitoring)

### Conditions Manageable at Home
- Common cold with mild symptoms
- Minor muscle soreness after exercise
- Mild headache responding to over-the-counter analgesics
- Mild fever below 38°C in healthy adults without other symptoms
- Minor skin irritation or contact dermatitis

### When to Escalate Home Conditions
- Symptoms persisting beyond 5-7 days without improvement
- New symptoms developing while on home management
- Fever that was improving but returns
- Any symptom causing significant concern or distress
