## CARDIAC CONDITIONS

### Heart Attack (Myocardial Infarction)
- category: cardiac
- condition: heart_attack
- Common Symptoms: crushing chest pain, pain radiating to left arm or jaw, shortness of breath, cold sweats, nausea, lightheadedness
- Risk Factors: age over 45, smoking, high blood pressure, high cholesterol, diabetes, obesity, family history of heart disease
- Severity Indicators: persistent chest pressure lasting more than 5 minutes, pain spreading to multiple areas
- Emergency Signs: sudden severe chest pain, loss of consciousness, inability to breathe, blue lips
- General Precautions: call emergency services immediately, do not drive, chew aspirin if not allergic, rest completely

### Angina
- category: cardiac
- condition: angina
- Common Symptoms: chest tightness or pressure triggered by exertion, pain relieved by rest, mild shortness of breath
- Risk Factors: coronary artery disease, high blood pressure, high cholesterol, diabetes, smoking
- Severity Indicators: symptoms occurring at rest (unstable angina), increasing frequency of episodes
- Emergency Signs: chest pain at rest lasting more than 20 minutes, new onset severe angina
- General Precautions: avoid strenuous activity during episodes, take prescribed medications, follow up with cardiologist

### Arrhythmia
- category: cardiac
- condition: arrhythmia
- Common Symptoms: palpitations, fluttering in chest, rapid or slow heartbeat, dizziness, fainting
- Risk Factors: heart disease, high blood pressure, thyroid disorders, electrolyte imbalance, caffeine excess
- Severity Indicators: fainting episodes, chest pain accompanying palpitations, very rapid or very slow pulse
- Emergency Signs: sudden loss of consciousness, no detectable pulse, extreme chest pain
- General Precautions: avoid stimulants, monitor pulse regularly, take medications as prescribed

## NEUROLOGICAL CONDITIONS

### Stroke (Cerebrovascular Accident)
- category: neurological
- condition: stroke
- Common Symptoms: sudden weakness or numbness on one side of body, facial drooping on one side, difficulty speaking or understanding, sudden severe headache, blurred or loss of vision in one eye, loss of balance
- Risk Factors: high blood pressure, atrial fibrillation, diabetes, smoking, high cholesterol, prior TIA, age over 65
- Severity Indicators: FAST signs present (Face drooping, Arm weakness, Speech difficulty, Time to call emergency), sudden onset within minutes
- Emergency Signs: any single FAST sign, sudden unconsciousness, seizure following neurological symptoms
- General Precautions: call emergency services immediately, note exact time symptoms started, do not give food or water, time is brain tissue

### Transient Ischemic Attack (TIA)
- category: neurological
- condition: tia
- Common Symptoms: brief episode of weakness, numbness, speech difficulty or vision changes lasting minutes to hours, full recovery afterward
- Risk Factors: same as stroke — hypertension, diabetes, atrial fibrillation, smoking
- Severity Indicators: multiple TIA episodes, symptoms recurring within 24 hours
- Emergency Signs: TIA symptoms lasting more than 1 hour should be treated as stroke
- General Precautions: seek urgent evaluation even if symptoms resolve, high risk of full stroke within 48 hours

### Migraine
- category: neurological
- condition: migraine
- Common Symptoms: severe throbbing headache usually one-sided, nausea, vomiting, sensitivity to light and sound, visual aura (zigzag lines, blind spots)
- Risk Factors: family history, hormonal changes, stress, sleep disturbances, certain foods (caffeine, alcohol, aged cheese)
- Severity Indicators: headache with fever and stiff neck (may suggest meningitis), headache after head injury
- Emergency Signs: sudden thunderclap headache (worst of life), headache with confusion or seizure, headache with fever and rash
- General Precautions: rest in dark quiet room, stay hydrated, identify and avoid triggers

### Meningitis
- category: neurological
- condition: meningitis
- Common Symptoms: severe headache, stiff neck, high fever, sensitivity to light, nausea, vomiting, altered consciousness
- Risk Factors: age under 5 or over 65, weakened immune system, living in close quarters, recent upper respiratory infection
- Severity Indicators: rapid onset within hours, petechial or purpuric rash (non-blanching)
- Emergency Signs: non-blanching rash, altered consciousness, seizure, signs appearing within hours
- General Precautions: treat as medical emergency, requires immediate hospital admission and antibiotics

## HEPATIC (LIVER) CONDITIONS

### Jaundice
- category: hepatic
- condition: jaundice
- Common Symptoms: yellow discoloration of skin and whites of eyes, dark tea-colored urine, pale clay-colored stool, itching, fatigue, abdominal pain in upper right quadrant
- Risk Factors: liver disease, excessive alcohol use, viral hepatitis infection, bile duct obstruction, hemolytic anemia
- Severity Indicators: rapidly worsening jaundice, confusion or altered mental state (hepatic encephalopathy), bleeding tendency
- Emergency Signs: jaundice with severe confusion, uncontrolled bleeding, signs of liver failure
- General Precautions: avoid alcohol completely, stay hydrated, seek urgent evaluation for cause

### Viral Hepatitis (A, B, C)
- category: hepatic
- condition: hepatitis
- Common Symptoms: fatigue, nausea, loss of appetite, jaundice, dark urine, abdominal discomfort, low-grade fever
- Risk Factors: Hepatitis A: contaminated food/water, travel to endemic areas; Hepatitis B/C: unprotected sex, sharing needles, blood exposure
- Severity Indicators: progressive jaundice, signs of liver failure, prolonged illness beyond 6 weeks
- Emergency Signs: hepatic encephalopathy (confusion, asterixis), coagulopathy (easy bruising, bleeding)
- General Precautions: rest, avoid alcohol and hepatotoxic medications, maintain hydration, isolation precautions for Hepatitis A

### Cirrhosis
- category: hepatic
- condition: cirrhosis
- Common Symptoms: fatigue, easy bruising, swollen abdomen (ascites), leg swelling, jaundice, confusion, spider angiomas on skin
- Risk Factors: chronic alcohol use, chronic viral hepatitis, non-alcoholic fatty liver disease, autoimmune hepatitis
- Severity Indicators: ascites, variceal bleeding, hepatic encephalopathy, spontaneous bacterial peritonitis
- Emergency Signs: vomiting blood (variceal hemorrhage), sudden worsening confusion, signs of infection with ascites
- General Precautions: complete alcohol abstinence, low-sodium diet, regular monitoring by hepatologist

## RESPIRATORY CONDITIONS

### Pneumonia
- category: respiratory
- condition: pneumonia
- Common Symptoms: productive cough (yellow or green sputum), fever and chills, shortness of breath, chest pain worsening with breathing, fatigue
- Risk Factors: age over 65 or under 2, smoking, chronic lung disease, weakened immune system, recent viral infection
- Severity Indicators: oxygen saturation below 94%, high fever above 39.5°C, confusion in elderly
- Emergency Signs: severe breathing difficulty at rest, cyanosis (blue lips), altered consciousness, high fever with rigors
- General Precautions: complete antibiotic course, rest, stay hydrated, monitor oxygen saturation

### Asthma
- category: respiratory
- condition: asthma
- Common Symptoms: wheezing, shortness of breath, chest tightness, dry cough especially at night or early morning, symptoms triggered by allergens or exercise
- Risk Factors: family history, allergies, eczema, exposure to smoke or pollution, childhood respiratory infections
- Severity Indicators: symptoms not relieved by rescue inhaler, frequent nighttime symptoms, decreasing peak flow readings
- Emergency Signs: severe breathing difficulty with no response to inhaler, unable to speak in full sentences, cyanosis
- General Precautions: avoid known triggers, carry rescue inhaler, use preventive inhaler as prescribed, have action plan

### Pulmonary Embolism
- category: respiratory
- condition: pulmonary_embolism
- Common Symptoms: sudden shortness of breath, sharp chest pain worsening with breathing, rapid heart rate, coughing up blood, leg swelling and pain (DVT source)
- Risk Factors: prolonged immobility, recent surgery, cancer, oral contraceptives, prior DVT or PE, inherited clotting disorders
- Severity Indicators: oxygen saturation drop, hemodynamic instability, massive PE with shock
- Emergency Signs: sudden severe shortness of breath, hypotension with tachycardia, loss of consciousness
- General Precautions: treat as medical emergency, requires immediate anticoagulation or thrombolysis

## GASTROINTESTINAL CONDITIONS

### Appendicitis
- category: gastrointestinal
- condition: appendicitis
- Common Symptoms: pain starting around navel moving to lower right abdomen, nausea, vomiting, loss of appetite, low-grade fever, pain worsening with movement
- Risk Factors: age 10-30 years most common, family history, dietary factors
- Severity Indicators: severe localized right lower quadrant tenderness, rebound tenderness (peritoneal signs), high fever
- Emergency Signs: sudden relief of pain followed by spreading abdominal pain (perforation), rigid abdomen, high fever
- General Precautions: treat as surgical emergency, do not eat or drink, do not take laxatives or pain medication before evaluation

### Peptic Ulcer Disease
- category: gastrointestinal
- condition: peptic_ulcer
- Common Symptoms: burning or gnawing stomach pain between meals or at night, pain relieved by food or antacids, nausea, bloating, dark tarry stools (if bleeding)
- Risk Factors: H. pylori infection, regular NSAID use, smoking, stress, alcohol use
- Severity Indicators: persistent pain unrelieved by antacids, black tarry stools, vomiting blood
- Emergency Signs: sudden severe abdominal pain (perforation), vomiting blood, black tarry stools indicating significant bleeding
- General Precautions: avoid NSAIDs and aspirin, reduce alcohol, eliminate smoking, complete H. pylori treatment course

## ENDOCRINE CONDITIONS

### Type 2 Diabetes
- category: endocrine
- condition: type2_diabetes
- Common Symptoms: increased thirst and urination, unexplained weight loss or gain, fatigue, blurred vision, slow-healing wounds, frequent infections, numbness in hands or feet
- Risk Factors: obesity, sedentary lifestyle, family history, age over 45, gestational diabetes history, prediabetes
- Severity Indicators: blood sugar above 300 mg/dL, signs of diabetic ketoacidosis or hyperosmolar state
- Emergency Signs: blood sugar very high with vomiting (DKA), extreme confusion with very high sugar, blood sugar below 70 with confusion (hypoglycemia)
- General Precautions: regular blood sugar monitoring, medication adherence, regular foot inspection, healthy diet and exercise

### Hypoglycemia (Low Blood Sugar)
- category: endocrine
- condition: hypoglycemia
- Common Symptoms: shakiness, sweating, rapid heartbeat, hunger, dizziness, pallor, confusion, difficulty concentrating
- Risk Factors: diabetes medications, prolonged fasting, excessive alcohol, insulinoma, certain medications
- Severity Indicators: blood sugar below 70 mg/dL, progressing confusion, inability to self-treat
- Emergency Signs: loss of consciousness, seizure due to severe hypoglycemia
- General Precautions: consume 15g fast-acting carbohydrate immediately, recheck in 15 minutes, if unconscious call emergency services

### Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA)
- category: endocrine
- condition: dka
- Common Symptoms: excessive thirst, frequent urination, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, fruity-smelling breath, confusion, deep rapid breathing
- Risk Factors: Type 1 diabetes, missed insulin doses, infection or illness triggering high blood sugar, newly diagnosed diabetes
- Severity Indicators: blood sugar above 250 mg/dL with ketones in urine, altered mental status, rapid breathing (Kussmaul breathing)
- Emergency Signs: altered consciousness, extreme dehydration, pH below 7.3 on blood gas
- General Precautions: treat as medical emergency, requires IV fluids and insulin, call emergency services

## MUSCULOSKELETAL CONDITIONS

### Fracture
- category: musculoskeletal
- condition: fracture
- Common Symptoms: sudden sharp pain at injury site, swelling and bruising, deformity or abnormal position, inability to bear weight or use limb, tenderness on palpation
- Risk Factors: trauma or injury, osteoporosis, age over 65, contact sports, vitamin D deficiency
- Severity Indicators: open fracture (bone visible), suspected spine or hip fracture, neurovascular compromise (numbness, no pulse distal to fracture)
- Emergency Signs: open fracture, spinal fracture with neurological symptoms, pelvic fracture with shock
- General Precautions: immobilize injured area, do not attempt to realign bone, apply ice wrapped in cloth, seek X-ray evaluation

### Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT)
- category: musculoskeletal
- condition: dvt
- Common Symptoms: swelling in one leg, calf pain or tenderness, warmth and redness over the affected area, leg feels heavier than normal
- Risk Factors: prolonged immobility, recent surgery or hospitalization, cancer, oral contraceptives, pregnancy, prior DVT, inherited clotting disorders
- Severity Indicators: significant swelling with marked discoloration, symptoms progressing rapidly
- Emergency Signs: sudden onset shortness of breath after leg swelling (pulmonary embolism), massive leg swelling with blue discoloration (phlegmasia)
- General Precautions: avoid prolonged immobility, seek urgent evaluation, requires anticoagulation treatment

## INFECTIOUS / SYSTEMIC CONDITIONS

### Sepsis
- category: systemic
- condition: sepsis
- Common Symptoms: high fever or hypothermia, rapid heart rate above 90 bpm, rapid breathing above 20 per minute, confusion or altered mental state, extreme fatigue, signs of infection source
- Risk Factors: recent infection, hospitalization, weakened immune system, age over 65 or under 1, chronic illness, invasive procedures
- Severity Indicators: qSOFA score: altered mentation + respiratory rate over 22 + systolic BP under 100
- Emergency Signs: signs of organ failure — no urine output, mottled skin, altered consciousness, blood pressure unresponsive to fluids
- General Precautions: medical emergency requiring immediate IV antibiotics and fluid resuscitation, call emergency services

### Dengue Fever
- category: systemic
- condition: dengue
- Common Symptoms: sudden high fever, severe headache, pain behind eyes, joint and muscle pain, skin rash appearing 2-5 days after fever, fatigue, mild bleeding from gums or nose
- Risk Factors: travel to tropical or subtropical regions, mosquito exposure, prior dengue infection (increases risk of severe dengue)
- Severity Indicators: fever above 39°C for more than 5 days, platelet count below 100,000, warning signs (abdominal pain, persistent vomiting, bleeding)
- Emergency Signs: severe abdominal pain, bleeding from multiple sites, plasma leakage with respiratory distress, rapid deterioration
- General Precautions: rest, hydration, paracetamol only (no aspirin or NSAIDs), mosquito bite prevention, monitor platelet count
