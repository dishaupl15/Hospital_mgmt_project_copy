## CHEST SYMPTOMS

### Chest Pain
- symptom: chest_pain
- Related Conditions: heart attack, angina, pulmonary embolism, pneumonia, pericarditis, GERD, costochondritis, panic attack
- Body System: cardiac, respiratory
- Red Flags: crushing quality, radiation to arm/jaw, associated sweating or nausea — suggests cardiac emergency
- Differentiation: cardiac pain is pressure-like and radiates; pleuritic pain is sharp and worsens with breathing; GERD pain is burning after meals

### Shortness of Breath
- symptom: shortness_of_breath
- Related Conditions: asthma, pneumonia, pulmonary embolism, heart failure, anemia, anxiety, COPD
- Body System: respiratory, cardiac
- Red Flags: sudden onset at rest, inability to speak in sentences, cyanosis (blue lips or fingertips)
- Differentiation: cardiac dyspnea worsens lying flat; asthma has wheeze; PE has sudden onset with pleuritic pain

### Palpitations
- symptom: palpitations
- Related Conditions: arrhythmia, atrial fibrillation, hyperthyroidism, anxiety, anemia, dehydration
- Body System: cardiac, endocrine
- Red Flags: palpitations with syncope or chest pain, very fast or very irregular pulse
- Differentiation: thyroid-related is continuous; anxiety-related follows stress; arrhythmia may occur at rest

## NEUROLOGICAL SYMPTOMS

### Sudden Unilateral Weakness
- symptom: unilateral_weakness
- Related Conditions: stroke, TIA, Todd's paresis after seizure, complex migraine, spinal cord lesion
- Body System: neurological
- Red Flags: sudden onset, face plus arm plus leg on same side, associated speech difficulty — FAST criteria for stroke
- Differentiation: stroke is sudden and maximal at onset; migraine aura is gradual and spreads over minutes

### Severe Headache
- symptom: severe_headache
- Related Conditions: migraine, meningitis, subarachnoid hemorrhage, hypertensive crisis, intracranial mass, tension headache
- Body System: neurological
- Red Flags: thunderclap headache (worst ever, sudden onset), headache with fever and stiff neck, headache after head trauma
- Differentiation: migraine has aura and photophobia; meningitis has fever and neck stiffness; SAH is sudden thunderclap

### Speech Difficulty
- symptom: speech_difficulty
- Related Conditions: stroke, TIA, Bell's palsy, complex migraine, brain tumor, severe hypoglycemia
- Body System: neurological
- Red Flags: sudden onset with facial weakness or arm weakness — stroke until proven otherwise
- Differentiation: Broca's aphasia (cannot produce speech); Wernicke's (produces nonsensical speech); dysarthria (slurred but coherent)

### Confusion / Altered Mental Status
- symptom: confusion
- Related Conditions: sepsis, meningitis, stroke, severe hypoglycemia, hyponatremia, hepatic encephalopathy, UTI in elderly, drug toxicity
- Body System: neurological, systemic
- Red Flags: acute onset confusion in elderly, confusion with fever, confusion with neurological signs
- Differentiation: metabolic causes are often fluctuating; structural (stroke, tumor) are more focal

## ABDOMINAL SYMPTOMS

### Abdominal Pain — Right Upper Quadrant
- symptom: ruq_pain
- Related Conditions: cholecystitis, cholelithiasis, hepatitis, liver abscess, right-sided pneumonia
- Body System: gastrointestinal, hepatic
- Red Flags: severe pain with fever and jaundice (Charcot's triad), Murphy's sign positive
- Differentiation: biliary colic is colicky and episodic; cholecystitis is constant with fever; hepatitis has gradual onset with jaundice

### Abdominal Pain — Right Lower Quadrant
- symptom: rlq_pain
- Related Conditions: appendicitis, ovarian cyst or torsion (females), ectopic pregnancy (females), inguinal hernia, Crohn's disease, mesenteric adenitis
- Body System: gastrointestinal
- Red Flags: rebound tenderness, guarding, fever with migration from peri-umbilical to RLQ — appendicitis until proven otherwise
- Differentiation: appendicitis pain migrates and worsens; ovarian torsion is sudden and severe; Crohn's has chronic history

### Jaundice (Yellow Discoloration)
- symptom: jaundice
- Related Conditions: viral hepatitis, alcoholic liver disease, biliary obstruction, hemolytic anemia, liver cirrhosis, pancreatic cancer
- Body System: hepatic
- Red Flags: rapidly progressive jaundice, jaundice with altered consciousness, painless jaundice with weight loss (suggests malignancy)
- Differentiation: pre-hepatic (hemolysis) — dark urine, no pale stool; hepatic — both; post-hepatic — dark urine AND pale stool

### Nausea and Vomiting
- symptom: nausea_vomiting
- Related Conditions: gastroenteritis, peptic ulcer, appendicitis, intestinal obstruction, pancreatitis, migraine, inner ear disorders, pregnancy
- Body System: gastrointestinal
- Red Flags: vomiting blood or coffee-ground material, projectile vomiting, vomiting with severe headache or abdominal rigidity
- Differentiation: projectile suggests obstruction; blood in vomit suggests GI bleed; with headache suggests raised intracranial pressure

## SKIN SYMPTOMS

### Yellowing of Skin and Eyes (Icterus)
- symptom: icterus
- Related Conditions: hepatitis A/B/C, biliary obstruction, cirrhosis, hemolytic conditions, Gilbert's syndrome
- Body System: hepatic
- Red Flags: rapid onset in adults, associated dark urine and pale stools, associated abdominal pain and fever
- Differentiation: see jaundice above

### Skin Rash with Fever
- symptom: rash_with_fever
- Related Conditions: dengue, meningococcemia, measles, rubella, drug reaction, sepsis, Rocky Mountain spotted fever
- Body System: systemic
- Red Flags: non-blanching petechial or purpuric rash — meningococcemia emergency
- Differentiation: dengue rash appears after fever peak; meningococcemia progresses rapidly with shock; measles starts on face

### Itching (Pruritus) Without Rash
- symptom: pruritus_without_rash
- Related Conditions: cholestasis (liver/bile duct disease), chronic kidney disease, thyroid disorders, polycythemia vera, lymphoma, iron deficiency
- Body System: hepatic, systemic
- Red Flags: generalized pruritus with jaundice (cholestatic), pruritus with night sweats and weight loss (lymphoma)
- Differentiation: cholestatic pruritus worse at night and on palms/soles; uremic pruritus in renal failure patients

## SYSTEMIC SYMPTOMS

### High Fever
- symptom: high_fever
- Related Conditions: bacterial infection, viral infection, dengue, malaria, sepsis, drug reaction, autoimmune disease
- Body System: systemic
- Red Flags: fever above 40°C, fever with altered consciousness, fever with non-blanching rash, fever with stiff neck
- Differentiation: bacterial infections tend to have higher and more sustained fever; viral fevers are often self-limiting

### Fatigue and Weakness
- symptom: fatigue_weakness
- Related Conditions: anemia, hypothyroidism, diabetes, depression, chronic infection, heart failure, chronic kidney disease, malignancy
- Body System: systemic, endocrine
- Red Flags: sudden severe fatigue, fatigue with weight loss and night sweats, fatigue with new neurological symptoms
- Differentiation: endocrine fatigue is gradual; cardiac fatigue worsens with exertion; anemia fatigue accompanies pallor

### Swollen Lymph Nodes
- symptom: lymphadenopathy
- Related Conditions: viral infection (EBV, CMV), bacterial infection, HIV, lymphoma, leukemia, autoimmune disease
- Body System: systemic
- Red Flags: hard fixed lymph nodes, generalized lymphadenopathy with weight loss and night sweats (B symptoms), rapidly enlarging nodes
- Differentiation: reactive nodes (infection) are tender and soft; malignant nodes are hard, non-tender, and fixed

## MUSCULOSKELETAL SYMPTOMS

### Joint Pain (Arthralgia)
- symptom: joint_pain
- Related Conditions: osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, gout, dengue, viral arthritis, reactive arthritis, lupus
- Body System: musculoskeletal
- Red Flags: hot swollen joint with fever (septic arthritis), joint pain with systemic symptoms (fever, rash, weight loss)
- Differentiation: osteoarthritis — morning stiffness under 30 min, weight-bearing joints; RA — morning stiffness over 1 hour, symmetric small joints

### Leg Swelling
- symptom: leg_swelling
- Related Conditions: DVT, heart failure, kidney disease, liver disease (hypoalbuminemia), venous insufficiency, lymphedema, cellulitis
- Body System: musculoskeletal, cardiac
- Red Flags: unilateral leg swelling with calf pain and warmth (DVT), bilateral leg swelling with shortness of breath (heart failure)
- Differentiation: unilateral = DVT or cellulitis or injury; bilateral = systemic cause (cardiac, renal, hepatic)
